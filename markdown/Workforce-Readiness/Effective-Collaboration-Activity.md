# Effective Collaboration Activity

## Objective

1. Work collaboratively to solve a complex problem
2. Identify principles of effective group collaboration

## Your Task

Your team has been hired as an independent software consultants for the company MindForge. They are creating an AI-driven mental health, productivity, and academic companion app designed specifically for university students. Their mission statement is "To empower every student to achieve peak academic performance and unwavering mental well-being through uncompromising, data-driven AI."

Your task is to rank these 15 features in **7 minutes** from most critical to least important, keeping a limited budget and development timeline in mind. 

- **End-to-End Encryption for All Personal Data:** Complete privacy for user journals and chats, meaning not even the company can see the data or use it to train future AI models.
- **Automated "Crisis Alert" System:** An algorithm that detects keywords related to self-harm or violence and automatically pings campus security and emergency contacts with the user's location.
- **Freemium Tier with Targeted Ads:** A free version of the app supported by ads targeted to the user based on their study habits and stress logs to ensure the company generates immediate revenue.
- **AI Essay & Assignment Outliner:** A feature that reads a professor's prompt and generates a highly detailed, personalized outline and thesis for the student to follow.
- **Anonymous Peer Support Chat Rooms:** Unmoderated, location-based chat rooms where students from the same university can vent and support each other anonymously.
- **Gamified "Study Hustle" Leaderboard:** A public, campus-wide ranking system showing which students have logged the most focus hours in the app each week, complete with badges and rewards.
- **Direct University LMS Integration:** An API connection to systems like Canvas or Blackboard that automatically pulls in a student's grades, upcoming deadlines, and professor feedback.
- **Premium 24/7 Human Therapist Access:** A high-cost subscription tier that allows users to bypass the AI and text directly with licensed human therapists at any time.
- **"Hardcore Focus" App Blocker:** A deep-level operating system integration that locks the student out of social media, texts, and games during scheduled study blocks, which cannot be bypassed until the timer ends.
- **Data Monetization Engine:** A backend feature that aggregates and anonymizes student stress and workload data to sell as reports to university administrators, creating a massive secondary revenue stream.
- **Customizable 3D AI Avatars:** A purely cosmetic feature allowing users to design and dress their AI companion, complete with unlockable microtransaction outfits to boost user retention.
- **Offline Mode:** Full functionality for the calendar, to-do lists, and journaling features even when the student has no cellular or Wi-Fi connection.
- **Neurodivergent Accessibility Suite:** High-quality voice-to-text journaling, screen readers, and customizable UI colors/fonts designed specifically for students with ADHD, Dyslexia, and Autism.
- **Wearable Tech Health Integration:** The ability to sync with Apple Watches and Fitbits to correlate the user's heart rate and sleep patterns with their study habits and stress levels.
- **Open Third-Party API:** Allowing outside developers to build plugins, widgets, and extensions for the app to rapidly grow its ecosystem, but increasing potential security vulnerabilities.

## Press Pause

After 7 minutes, answer the following questions as a group. 

1. What was your group’s methodology?
2. How were disagreements resolved?
3. Who has been talking the most and who has been talking the least? 
4. What are your group's values? Name up to 5 principles your group values that you want to keep in mind as you move forward. Think about what kind of environments have been most productive in your past collaborative experiences.
5. What strategies to you want to try in the next phase of the activity to uphold your values?


## Try Some Strategies

Keeping your strategies in mind, work in groups for the next **ten minutes**. Then answer these questions.

1. What was your group’s methodology?
2. How were disagreements resolved?
3. Who has been talking the most and who has been talking the least? 
4. How effective were your strategies in upholding your values?
