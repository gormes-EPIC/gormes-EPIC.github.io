## Career Research Project
### Objective
Students will research a real-world career, analyze its required skills and education, and present their findings in a professional format
### Part 1: Identify a Job

1. Your task is to identify an entry level job in a field you are interested in pursuing.
    1. Choose a job you would like to have. Research the entry level positions that feed into that job. 
    2. There will probably be multiple different options for you to choose from.
2. If you are having difficulty, find someone in a similar role on LinkedIn and look back in their previous experience

Once you have the name of an entry level role, move to step 2.  

### Part 2: Analyze Job Field

Your task is to analyze your job and identify common requirements and skills. 
1. Find at least three job postings for your job. 
2. From the postings identify the following. 
    - Job title and field
    - Description of typical duties and responsibilities
    - Common tools, languages, or technologies used
    - Typical education or certification requirements
    - Key skills (technical and soft)
    - Average salary range and job outlook
    - Example companies or organizations that hire for this role
    - How will AI impact this role?
3. To help you, [roadmap.sh](https://roadmap.sh/) is a great place to look for lists of skills required for different computer science careers.
4. Extra credit: Reach out to 3 people who currently hold this role at a company you would be interested in. They may not respond, but if they do use this opportunity to get first-hand knowledge about the job. Ask them about their experience, their job history, required skills etc.

### Part 3: Create a Visual
Create a visual representation of what you learned. Include the information you took notes on in the previous section. 

### Part 4: Present your Project
During class, you will present your visual. Use the strong presentation skills we identified in the in-class video.
