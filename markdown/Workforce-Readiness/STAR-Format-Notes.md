# Answering Interview Questions in a STAR Format

<img src="/assets/star-format.png">

The **STAR format** will help you answer interview questions thoroughly and highlight your specific impacts.

**STAR** stands for:
- **Situation:** Give context to your situation
- **Task:** Elaborate on the challenge and your role
- **Action:** Explain how you handled the situation and overcame the challenge
- **Result:** What you achieved in the process


As an example, let's pretend you are in and interview and were just asked: "Tell me about a time when comunication was critical to your team accomplishing a task".

To answer in a STAR format, you might reply: "One time I was working on a team project in my software engineering class. We needed to develop an interactive puzzle for a client escape room company. At the beginning of the project, we were confused about the specifications and were unable to come up with well-developed prototypes. As a group we came up with a list of questions that would help us as program designers, but were non-technical enough the client was able to give us thorough answers. The client responsed and gave us enough detail to move forward with our prototypes. As we continued the build, we communicated heavily with the client to make sure that our project was in line with their vision. As a result, we were able to deliver a final product the client was happy with."

Breaking down our response, we can see all 4 components of a STAR response
- **Situation:** One time I was working on a team project in my software engineering class. We needed to develop an interactive puzzle for a client escape room company. 
- **Task:** At the beginning of the project, we were confused about the specifications and were unable to come up with well-developed prototypes. 
- **Action:** As a group we came up with a list of questions that would help us as program designers, but were non-technical enough the client was able to give us thorough answers. The client responsed and gave us enough detail to move forward with our prototypes.
- **Result:** As we continued the build, we communicated heavily with the client to make sure that our project was in line with their vision. As a result, we were able to deliver a final product the client was happy with.

As you give your response, you want to highlight the **action you or your team took specifically** so it is most effective in demonstrating your skills. Remember that these answers are meant to prove that you have the skills you have listed on your resume and are able to communicate them to your interviewer effectively.
