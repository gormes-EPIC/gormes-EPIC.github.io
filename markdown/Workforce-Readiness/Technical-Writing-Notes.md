# Principles of Technical Writing

## Audience and Purpose

Before you get started, consider your audience and purpose of the document. Who are you trying to comunicate with and what level of skill do they have? You can use this information to tailor your language and level of depth to align with their level of understanding. In this class, you can assume the reader has some computer science knowledge, but is unfamiliar with your particular project. It is always best to assume the audience is less skilled than you think. 

## Writing Style

Use a neutral, academic tone and write confidently. Remember that you are the expert on your topic! You want to write impersonally and focus on the content. Construct your sentences in active voice and avoid slang and unnececessary jargon.

## Clarity and Organization

Clean and simple language is important for communicating complex information. One of the most important purposes of technical writing is to connect with an audience with little experience or knowledge about a topic. It is always best practice to use simple, straight-forward language and organize content in a logical way. Use formatting like headers, bullet points, numbered lists, visual aids, and tables to organize information and enhance clarity. 

## Correctness

Always use correct grammar and puncuation. Be sure to proofread your work! Your writing represents you to your readers so you want to make a good impression.
