# Developing Your Elevator Pitch

In this assignment you will develop a 30-60 response to the question "tell me about yourself." Assume you are being interviewed for a position in your chosen industry.


## Getting Started

1. Take notes on each of the following questions. 


- What is your professional/educational background?
    - Where are you currently employed?
    - Where did you graduate from? If you are a full time student, include your institution, major, and graduation year?
    - What industries have you worked in, and what roles have you held?
    - What experiences have shaped your career the most?

- What are your key skills or strengths?
    - What specific skills or abilities do you excel in?
    - How do those skills set you apart from others in your field?


- What accomplishments are you most proud of?
    - Can you think of a specific project or achievement that showcases your capabilities?
    - How did you overcome challenges or contribute to the success of a team or company?


- What motivates you?
    - What drives you in your work or career?
    - Are there certain aspects of a role, company, or industry that you are particularly passionate about?


- What makes you unique?
    - What distinguishes you from others in your field or industry?
    - Do you have any personal experiences, interests, or perspectives that influence your professional life?



2. Using your notes create your 30-60 second(75-150 words) elevator pitch. **First, practice your pitch with a peer and refine it with their feedback.** Then, you can either record your pitch on video or give your pitch in class.

*Example: My name is Gwyneth Ormes, and I am a high school senior graduating in 2018. Outside of my regular classes, I have a strong passion for computer science and leadership. I currently compete in CyberPatriot, an international cybersecurity competition, and I recently earned my Girl Scout Gold Award by creating a camp that introduces fifth-grade girls in my community to computer science. Beyond tech, I love staying active and creative. I work as a camp counselor for a high ropes course, and I serve as the costume crew head for my school’s theatre program.  I am a dedicated and driven student and I am excited to pursue computer science in college, and eventually as my career.*

## Rubric
- **30 points** - Delivered a in-depth, well-rehersed, and professional pitch about themselves 
- **15 points** - Delivered a pitch but did not cover a majority of the topics above or was unprofessionally presented
- **0 points** - Missing or incomplete
