# Writing a Cover Letter

1. Read the following job description and highlight the required skills and core values they are looking for.

## High School Software Engineering Intern
Are you a high school student who loves to code and wants to know why one solution is better than another? We are looking for a motivated High School Software Engineering Intern to join our engineering team. In this role, you won't just be fixing typos or writing basic scripts. You will dive into the core of computer science by evaluating different data structures and algorithms to see how they apply to real-world problems. Using Python and Java, you will help our team benchmark solutions, optimize existing code, and build prototypes that make our software run faster and smarter.
### What You Will Do
- Evaluate & Analyze: Research and test various data structures (e.g., HashMaps, Trees, LinkedLists) and algorithms to determine the most efficient approach for specific technical challenges.
- Write Code: Develop, test, and debug software solutions using Python and Java.
Benchmark Performance: Help measure trade-offs (like speed vs. memory usage) when applying different algorithmic solutions to a given problem.
- Collaborate & Learn: Participate in team stand-ups, code reviews, and pair programming sessions with senior engineers.
- Document: Clearly explain your findings and the reasoning behind why a specific data structure or algorithm was chosen for a task.
What We Are Looking For
### Education & Technical Skills
- Education: Currently enrolled in high school (Juniors and Seniors preferred) with an interest in pursuing Computer Science or a related STEM field.
- Coding: Comfortable writing, testing, and debugging code in both Python and Java.
- Computer Science Fundamentals: Basic familiarity with foundational data structures (arrays, lists, dictionaries/maps) and a strong desire to learn about more complex ones.
### Core Competencies
- Critical Thinking & Problem Solving: You have a natural curiosity for how things work under the hood. You can break down complex puzzles, think logically, and analyze the pros and cons of different solutions.
- Collaboration: You are a true team player who is excited to pair program, brainstorm with peers, and work effectively alongside experienced mentors.
- Communication: You can clearly articulate your thought process—both verbally and in written code documentation. Most importantly, you know how to share ideas and aren't afraid to proactively ask for help when you get stuck.
- Professionalism: You demonstrate reliability by managing your tasks responsibly, showing up on time, and bringing a positive, respectful, and eager-to-learn attitude to the workplace.
### What You Will Gain
- Real-World Experience: Move beyond classroom assignments and see how software engineering works in a professional environment.
- Mentorship: 1-on-1 guidance from experienced software engineers who are dedicated to helping you grow.
- Portfolio Building: Tangible projects and performance benchmarks you can add to your GitHub, resume, and college applications.

## Your Task 

2. Of the skills and values you highlighted, choose the top three you excel at. For each, come up with an example of you demonstrating that skill or value in your life. Write your answer in a STAR format.

3. Create a short introduction to introduce yourself and preface the three skills you will highlight in your letter. Your introduction should also talk about WHY you want to join the organization.
4. Create a short conclusion to reiterate your three skills and reinforce that you are the right candidate for this position.
