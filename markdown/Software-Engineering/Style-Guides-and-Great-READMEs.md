## Analyzing Writing Style Guides

A **style guide** is a document that lists a set of standards for writing, formatting, and designing content to ensure consistency in voice, tone, and visual identity for a company or brand. 

Companies will designate a specific style they want all written text, including developer documentation, to use. You as employee of the company must be able to write in their style. 

### How Is This Relevant To Me Right Now? 
 
When you write any document from an essay in language arts class to a technical README in this class, you are communicating something about yourself to your audience through your unique voice and tone. In a job or a college application, you can use voice and tone to affect how you come across to others. This includes all of the documentation you write on your portfolio (your GitHub profile). 

The key detail here is that you are able to control how you come across to employers or colleges based on your writing.
